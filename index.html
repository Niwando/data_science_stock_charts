Candle
A candle in a stock chart is a visual representation of the price movement of a stock during a particular time frame. It is made up of a rectangular body and two thin lines, known as wicks or shadows, that extend from the top and bottom of the body. The body represents the opening and closing prices of the stock during the time period, while the wicks show the highest and lowest prices that were reached.

Size
The size of a field of a stock on the stock map describes the average daily performance of the stock relative to other stocks. A stock field may be smaller than another if the average daily performance is lower than that of the other stock.
The size of a subfield within a stock field describes the frequency of a particular daily performance of that stock relative to the frequencies of other daily performances. This means that a smaller subfield indicates that the particular daily performance is less frequent compared to other daily performances of the same stock. The subfield size is determined by the number of occurrences of that specific daily performance within the total timeframe of the stock data.
